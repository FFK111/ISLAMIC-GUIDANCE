A Message for When You Feel Despair

Your feeling of hopelessness is a heavy burden, but know that in Islam, hope is an essential part of faith. Despair is a whisper from Shaytan, who wants you to forget the vastness of Allah's mercy.

Words from the Quran to Revive Your Hope

"O my sons, go and find out about Joseph and his brother and despair not of relief from Allah. Indeed, no one despairs of relief from Allah except the disbelieving people."

[Quran, 12:87]

A Message for an Overthinking Mind

Anxiety often comes from trying to carry tomorrow's burdens with today's strength. Islam teaches us the beautiful concept of Tawakkul—perfect trust in Allah's plan. Your role is to do your best today, and to entrust the results and the future to the One who has perfect knowledge.

Words from the Quran on Trusting Allah

وَمَن يَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ فَهُوَ حَسْبُهُ ۚ

"...And whoever relies upon Allah - then He is sufficient for him."

[Quran, 65:3]

Dua for Entrusting Your Affairs to Allah

حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ ۖ وَهُوَ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ

Allah is sufficient for me. There is no god but He. In Him I have placed my trust, and He is the Lord of the Mighty Throne.

[Sunan Abi Dawood 5081]

وَهُوَ مَعَكُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ ۚ

"...And He is with you wherever you are..." [Quran, 57:4]

A Message for When You Face a Crossroads

Indecision can be paralyzing. Islam provides a beautiful tool for this exact situation: Salat al-Istikharah (The Prayer of Seeking Guidance). You are not expected to have all the answers. Your role is to research your options, and then turn to the One who knows all things, asking Him to guide you to what is best.

The Complete Dua of Istikharah

After praying two non-obligatory rak'at, supplicate with:

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْتَخِيرُكَ بِعِلْمِكَ، وَأَسْتَقْدِرُكَ بِقُدْرَتِكَ، وَأَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ الْعَظِيمِ، فَإِنَّكَ تَقْدِرُ وَلاَ أَقْدِرُ، وَتَعْلَمُ وَلاَ أَعْلَمُ، وَأَنْتَ عَلاَّمُ الْغُيُوبِ، اللَّهُمَّ إِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الأَمْرَ خَيْرٌ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي فَاقْدُرْهُ لِي وَيَسِّرْهُ لِي، ثُمَّ بَارِكْ لِي فِيهِ، وَإِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الأَمْرَ شَرٌّ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي فَاصْرِفْهُ عَنِّي وَاصْرِفْنِي عَنْهُ، وَاقْدُرْ لِي الْخَيْرَ حَيْثُ كَانَ، ثُمَّ أَرْضِنِي بِهِ

"O Allah, I seek Your guidance by Your knowledge, and I seek ability by Your power, and I ask of Your great bounty. For You have power, I have none. And You know, I know not. You are the Knower of hidden things. O Allah, if You know that this matter [mention your matter here] is good for me in my religion, my livelihood and my affairs in the end, then ordain it for me, make it easy for me, and bless it for me. And if You know that this matter is bad for me in my religion, my livelihood and my affairs in the end, then turn it away from me and turn me away from it, and ordain for me the good wherever it may be and make me pleased with it."

[Sahih al-Bukhari 1166]

A Message for a Grieving Heart

Your sadness is seen and acknowledged by Allah. In Islam, there is great wisdom and reward in patience during times of hardship. Remember that every moment of grief, when met with patience, can be a means of purification and drawing closer to your Creator.

Words of Allah for the Patient

وَلَنَبْلُوَنَّكُم بِشَيْءٍ مِّنَ الْخَوْفِ وَالْجُوعِ وَنَقْصٍ مِّنَ الْأَمْوَالِ وَالْأَنفُسِ وَالثَّمَرَاتِ ۗ وَبَشِّرِ الصَّابِرِينَ. الَّذِينَ إِذَا أَصَابَتْهُم مُّصِيبَةٌ قَالُوا إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ

"And We will surely test you with something of fear and hunger and a loss of wealth and lives and fruits, but give good tidings to the patient, Who, when disaster strikes them, say, 'Indeed we belong to Allah, and indeed to Him we will return.'"

[Quran, 2:155-156]

Dua when afflicted with a calamity

إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ، اللَّهُمَّ أْجُرْنِي فِي مُصِيبَتِي، وَأَخْلِفْ لِي خَيْرًا مِنْهَا

We belong to Allah and to Him we return. O Allah, reward me for my affliction and replace it for me with something better.

[Sahih Muslim 918]

A Message for Times of Illness

When your health fails, it is a powerful reminder of our dependence on Allah, the Healer (Ash-Shafi). Every moment of pain or discomfort, when borne with patience, is a means by which Allah expiates sins. Your illness does not decrease your worth; in fact, it can elevate your status if you turn to Him.

Guidance from the Prophet (ﷺ)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "No fatigue, nor disease, nor sorrow, nor sadness, nor hurt, nor distress befalls a Muslim, even if it were the prick he receives from a thorn, but that Allah expiates some of his sins for that."

[Sahih al-Bukhari 5641]

Dua of Prophet Ayyub (Job)

A Message for a Heavy Heart

The feeling of guilt is a sign of a heart that is alive. It is a gift from Allah, calling you back to Him. Do not let Shaytan turn this guilt into despair. Instead, turn it into sincere repentance (Tawbah), for Allah's mercy is vaster than any sin you can imagine.

Words from the Quran on Repentance

قُلْ يَا عِبَادِيَ الَّذِينَ أَسْرَفُوا عَلَىٰ أَنفُسِهِمْ لَا تَقْنَطُوا مِن رَّحْمَةِ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ جَمِيعًا ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ

وَهُوَ مَعَكُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ ۚ

"...And He is with you wherever you are." [Quran, 57:4]

A Message to Cool the Heart

Jealousy (Hasad) is a fire that consumes one's good deeds. It stems from being displeased with the Decree of Allah. The cure is to practice contentment (Qana'ah) and to make dua for the person you feel jealous of. Ask Allah to bless them in what they have, and to grant you from His boundless grace.

Guidance from the Prophet (ﷺ)

"Beware of envy, for envy consumes good deeds just as fire consumes wood or grass."

[Sunan Abi Dawood 4903, Graded Hasan]

Dua for Protection from Envy

Recite the last two surahs of the Quran (Al-Falaq and An-Nas). Surah Al-Falaq says:

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ. مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ. وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ. وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ. وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ

"Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of daybreak, From the evil of that which He created, And from the evil of darkness when it settles, And from the evil of the blowers in knots, And from the evil of an envier when he envies.'"

[Quran, 113]

A Message for When You Feel Lost

A Message for Finding Your True Worth

Insecurity comes from measuring ourselves by the standards of this world. Your true worth in the sight of Allah is not based on your appearance, wealth, or status, but on your piety (Taqwa). Allah created you in the best of forms. Find your confidence in being a servant of the Most High.

Words from the Quran on Our Creation

"We have certainly created man in the best of stature."

[Quran, 95:4]

Guidance from the Prophet (ﷺ) on True Value

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Verily, Allah does not look at your appearance or your wealth, but He looks at your hearts and your deeds."

[Sahih Muslim 2564]

May Allah accept our good deeds and grant us peace in our hearts.
